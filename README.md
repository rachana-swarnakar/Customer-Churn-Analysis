<h3>Telco Customer Churn Exploratory Data Analysis</h3>

<h4>Problem</h4>
<p>Telecom companies often lose customers when they switch to other service providers. This is called customer churn. High churn means loss of revenue and lower customer loyalty. The goal of this project is to analyze telecom customer data and understand the main reasons why customers leave the service.</p>  

<h4>Data Approach</h4>

<p>The dataset was analyzed using Excel. Basic data cleaning was done to organize the dataset and make it easier to analyze. Different charts and visualizations were created to compare churn with factors such as tenure, contract type, internet service, payment method, and additional services. The analysis focused on identifying patterns that show which types of customers are more likely to churn.</p>

<h4>Key Findings</h4>
<p>

  - About 26% of customers left the service, showing a noticeable churn problem.
  -  Customers with short tenure (new customers) are more likely to churn.
  - Month-to-month contract users leave more often than customers with longer contracts.
  - Customers without extra services like online security or tech support show higher churn.
  - Users with fiber optic internet tend to churn more compared to other internet services.
  - Customers who pay using electronic checks have a higher churn rate.
</p>

<h4>Business Recommendations</h4>
<p>
  
-  Encourage customers to choose long-term contracts by offering discounts or benefits.
-  Provide bundled services like tech support and online security to increase customer value.
-  Improve service quality and customer experience for fiber optic users.
-  Focus on retaining new customers, especially during their first few months.
-  Offer better payment options and incentives to reduce churn among electronic check users.
</p>
<p>This project helps identify the main factors behind customer churn and provides useful insights for improving customer retention.</p>
